# Tech Support Subcommittee Charter

## Vision — Why We Exist

*A descriptive picture of the desired future state*

* Elders well-prepared for cognitive or physical challenges, with technology that enhances quality of life, independence, and social connection
* Caregivers empowered to support elders through technological changes
* A community where technology simplifies rather than complicates daily life

## Mission — What We Do

*Methods and strategies for achieving the vision*

* Document and share best practices for caregivers supporting elders with technology
* Provide accessible, user-friendly technology solutions tailored to elders' needs
* Establish a lending library of elder-friendly tech devices
* Conduct regular workshops and one-on-one training sessions for elders and caregivers
* Maintain a dedicated email address for support inquiries

## Values — Who We Are

*Shared ethics and principles guiding our work*

* **Empathy** — Understanding the unique challenges elders face in adapting to new technologies
* **Accessibility** — Ensuring all solutions are user-friendly and inclusive
* **Continuous Learning** — Staying current with technological and caregiving advances
* **Collaboration** — Partnering with caregivers, elders, and tech experts
* **Respect** — Honoring the dignity and autonomy of elders in every interaction

## Goals — Milestones & Metrics

*Specific, measurable objectives tracking progress toward the mission*

* **Q2 2026** — Develop and distribute a comprehensive caregiver guide on assisting elders with technology
* **Q3 2026** — Launch a lending library stocked with elder-friendly tech devices
* **Q4 2026** — Hold monthly workshops reaching at least 10 participants per session

## Open Items

*For future discussion*

* **Scope** — Does this committee support personal devices (phones, laptops), community-owned assets, or both? Clarifying this early prevents scope creep.
* **Membership** — Who serves on this subcommittee — residents only, or staff as well?
* **Meeting Cadence** — How often does the group meet? A standard schedule (e.g., "3rd Tuesday, monthly") sets clear expectations.
