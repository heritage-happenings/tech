## Tech Support Subcommittee — Request for Permission

Dear Jon, Hector, and Tom,

The lack of consistent, accessible tech support for residents remains an unresolved concern that directly affects quality of life. Research consistently shows that seniors who actively use technology experience better health outcomes, stronger social connections, and greater independence — making reliable tech support not just a convenience but a meaningful contributor to well-being.

Currently, Tess Goldman (a volunteer) is our pillar of tech support, backed by Carl Washington and often by Sean and Cortez. Archie also helps. But the number of residents needing support continues to grow. We need a sustainable, scalable solution.

### Challenges

* None of us, to my knowledge, have professional training in supporting tech devices for the elderly.
* Few of us have explored what phone manufacturers already offer to assist older adults.
* Volunteers and staff take different approaches to fixing things, which can cause confusion and conflict.

### Goals

* **Reliable access** — Ensure all residents can reach consistent tech support that meets their needs.
* **Documented practices** — Establish and share common procedures and training for volunteers and staff.
* **Proactive support** — Anticipate needs and provide resources before issues arise — large-key keyboards, large-button phones, tablets with simplified interfaces.

### Proposal

I have been discussing this with Claire Nuccio (our EmpowerMe Speech Therapist), Sean, and Carl. We have drafted a charter for a Tech Support Subcommittee outlining our vision, mission, and goals. I would welcome your feedback on it.

**Jon, Hector, and Tom** — I am asking for your permission to move forward and to enable Carl and Sean to participate. Your support and guidance would be invaluable.

Claire and I have started the early work. For the next quarter or two, we expect to handle nearly all of it ourselves; Carl and Sean would mostly be responding to emails. Business would be conducted by email and casual face-to-face encounters — I don't envisage significant meeting time (unless it's to celebrate).

I look forward to your feedback and I hope you will see the value in this initiative.
