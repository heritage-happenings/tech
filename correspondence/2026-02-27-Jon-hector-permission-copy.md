Tech Support Subcommittee Charter

Dear Jon, Hector, and Tom

* The lack of consistent, accessible tech support for residents remains an unresolved concern that directly affects their quality of life
* Research consistently shows that seniors who actively use technology experience better health outcomes, stronger social connections, and greater independence — making reliable tech support not just a convenience but a meaningful contributor to resident well-being

Currently Tess Goldman (a volunteer) is our pillar of tech support. She is supported by Carl Washington and quite often Sean and Cortez. Cortez and Aarvie are likely to help.

Nonetheless the number of residents needing support grows. The demand for tech support is increases. We need to ensure that we have a sustainable, scalable solution in place to meet this demand.

Several issues have been observed that could help us understand the problem better and guide us toward effective solutions:

As far as I know, none of us have any professional skills in tech devices for the elderly.
Few of us have actually looked to see what the phone manufacturers do to assist the young.
Different volunteers and staff have different ways of fixing things, which can cause conflict.

Several goals have been observed that could help us understand the problem better and guide us toward effective solutions:

We need to ensure that all residents have access to reliable, consistent tech support that meets their needs.
* Consistent support could could be achieved with standard procedures and training for volunteers and staff.

We need to ensure that our tech support is proactive, not just reactive — anticipating residents' needs and providing resources before issues arise.
* Keyboards with large keys, phones with large buttons, and tablets with simplified interfaces are examples of proactive solutions that could enhance residents' experience and reduce the need for reactive support.

With these intensions in mind, I have been in discussion with Claire Nuccio, our EmpowerMe Speech Therapist and also Sean and Carl about setting up a task force to address this issue. I have drafted a charter for a Tech Support Subcommittee that outlines our vision, mission, values, and goals for improving tech support for our residents. I would like to share this charter with you and get your feedback on it. I believe that by working together, we can create a more effective and sustainable tech support system that enhances the quality of life for our residents.

In particular, Jon and Hector, I would like to ask for your permission to move forward with this initiative and to enable Carl and Sean to participate in the Tech Support Subcommittee. Their support (and yours )and guidance would be invaluable as we work to improve tech support for our residents.

Claire and I have started to do the early work on this. Most of the business would be by email and perhaps some casual face-to-face encounters, but I don't envisage any significant meeting time (unless it's to celebrate).
