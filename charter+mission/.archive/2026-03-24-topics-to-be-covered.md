# Topics to Be Covered in Tech Support Sessions — Staff Survey

**A reminder:** Our focus is on helping elders (ages 80–100) maintain independence and connection, and empowering their caregivers to support them with technology.

The topics below are organized into three tiers based on expected frequency of support requests.

Feel free to scribble notes, add comments, and write in anything we've missed.


---

## Tier 1 — Core (expect these weekly)

### Staying Connected
- Video calling (Zoom, FaceTime, Skype) — setup, joining, troubleshooting audio/video
- Phone basics — contacts, voicemail, volume, answering calls
- Email — reading, composing, attachments, avoiding spam
- Messaging with family (WhatsApp, iMessage, Messenger)
- Photo sharing — sending and receiving photos with family

### Staying Safe
- Recognizing scams — phone, email, and text phishing
- Password basics — creation, storage, resets, password managers
- Avoiding dangerous links and pop-ups
- What to do when something goes wrong ("I clicked something bad")
- Privacy basics — what not to share online or on the phone

### Everyday Devices
- Tablets — navigation, app basics, charging, frozen screens
- Smartphones — core functions, settings, text size, volume
- TV and streaming — remote control help, input switching, Roku/Apple TV basics
- Laptop basics — power, Wi-Fi connection, browser, common stuck states

### Seeing & Hearing Better
- Making text and icons larger (display settings, zoom, magnification)
- Volume and audio adjustments across devices
- Bluetooth hearing aid pairing and troubleshooting
- Voice assistants (Alexa, Siri, Google) — setup and daily use for hands-free control
- Screen reader basics (VoiceOver, TalkBack)

### Staying Independent
- Telehealth — joining video appointments, patient portal navigation
- Online banking — logging in, checking balances, spotting fraud
- Prescription management — pharmacy apps, refill systems
- Grocery and meal delivery apps (Instacart, DoorDash)
- Ride-hailing basics (Uber, Lyft)
- Government services — Social Security, Medicare portals

---

## Tier 2 — Regular (expect these monthly)

### Hardware Support
- Laptops — basic maintenance, updates, performance issues
- Printers — setup, connectivity, paper jams, ink
- Peripherals — external keyboards, mice, monitors (especially for low vision)
- Arthritis-friendly input devices — trackballs, large-key keyboards, styluses
- Medical alert systems — pendant setup, testing, battery, false alarms
- Wearable devices — smartwatches (especially Apple Watch fall detection)

### Internet & Connectivity
- Wi-Fi troubleshooting — dropouts, reconnecting devices
- Modem/router basics — restarting, when to call the ISP
- Bluetooth pairing — headphones, speakers, hearing aids, keyboards

### Software & Settings
- Operating system basics — Windows and macOS navigation
- iOS and Android — settings, updates, storage management
- App installation and removal
- Software updates — when and how, handling restart prompts
- Browser basics — bookmarks, tabs, clearing cache

### Communication Beyond Basics
- Setting up new email accounts
- Managing contacts across devices
- Group video calls and family check-ins
- Shared calendars with caregivers or family

### Entertainment
- Streaming services — Netflix, Hulu, YouTube, PBS
- Music and audiobooks — Spotify, Apple Music, Audible, Libby
- E-books — Kindle, library apps
- Audio systems — connecting speakers, soundbars, headphones

### Data & Files
- Cloud storage basics — iCloud, Google Drive (especially photo backup)
- Backup strategies — what matters, how to automate
- Finding files — photos, downloads, documents
- Transferring photos from phone to computer or to family
