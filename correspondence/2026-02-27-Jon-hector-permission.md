## Tech Support Subcommittee Charter

Dear Jon, Hector, and Tom,

* The lack of consistent, accessible tech support for residents remains an unresolved concern that directly affects their quality of life.
* Research consistently shows that seniors who actively use technology experience better health outcomes, stronger social connections, and greater independence — making reliable tech support not just a convenience but a meaningful contributor to resident well-being.

Currently, Tess Goldman (a volunteer) is our pillar of tech support. She is supported by Carl Washington and quite often by Sean and Cortez. Archie is also likely to help.

Nonetheless, the number of residents needing support continues to grow. We need to ensure that we have a sustainable, scalable solution in place to meet this demand.

### Challenges

Several issues have been observed that could help us understand the problem and guide us toward effective solutions:

* As far as I know, none of us have any professional skills in tech devices for the elderly.
* Few of us have actually looked at what phone manufacturers do to assist older adults.
* Different volunteers and staff have different approaches to fixing things, which can cause confusion and conflict.

### Goals

* **Reliable access**: Ensure that all residents have access to consistent tech support that meets their needs.
* **Standardized practices**: Achieve consistency through standard procedures and training for volunteers and staff.
* **Proactive support**: Anticipate residents' needs and provide resources before issues arise — for example, keyboards with large keys, phones with large buttons, and tablets with simplified interfaces.

### Proposal

With these intentions in mind, I have been in discussion with Claire Nuccio, our EmpowerMe Speech Therapist, as well as Sean and Carl, about setting up a task force to address this issue. I have drafted a charter for a Tech Support Subcommittee that outlines our vision, mission, values, and goals for improving tech support for our residents. I would like to share this charter with you and get your feedback.

**Jon, Hector and Tom**: I would like to ask for your permission to move forward with this initiative and to enable Carl and Sean to participate in the Tech Support Subcommittee. Your support and guidance would be invaluable as we work to improve tech support for our residents.

Claire and I have started the early work on this. I expect that for the next quarter or two, Claire and I will do nearly all the necessary work, and that Carl and Sean will mostly be responding to emails. Most of the business would be conducted by email and perhaps some casual face-to-face encounters — I don't envisage any significant meeting time (unless it's to celebrate).

I look forward to your feedback. I hope you will see the value in this initiative and grant us permission to move forward.
